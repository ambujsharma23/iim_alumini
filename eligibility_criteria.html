IIM Alumni Startup Entrepreneurial Excellence Awards 2017 Categories



The IIM Alumni Startup Entrepreneurial Excellence Awards 2017 recognise and celebrate contributions of IIM Alumni's made to the evolving and developing Startup world. Only those founders or co-founders who have done excellent work in the startup community shall be felicitated both in Promising Startups and Startup Stars category. Startups from pan-India are welcome to participate in different award categories. These awards encourage and support entries which grow and enhance India's digital eco-system.

For Promising Startups, the date of incorporation of startups should be less than or equal to 5 years as on October 31, 2017 or the total revenue generated in terms of turnover from the date of incorporation should be less than or equal to Rs. 50 Crore whereas for Startup Stars, the date of incorporation of startups should be more than 5 years as on October 31, 2017 or the total revenue generated in terms of turnover from the date of incorporation should be more than Rs. 50 Crore. For Startup Stars, the new initiative for the corporate should not be more than 10 years as on October 31, 2017.

1. Citizen Services

Initiatives in the area of e-Governance through the effective use of ICT. Focus should be on how different projects have led to significant cost savings and also arresting revenue leakages in different government departments whether state or central.

2. Financial Services

India has seen many digital payment solutions deployment but the focus is on how these solutions have made significant contribution in terms of adoption, no of transactions and ease of use using ICT. Using the app, the monthly growth in terms of no of transactions for digital payment services and how it has helped in India moving towards a less cash economy.

3. Education

Focus is on innovative and sustainable technologies developed to improve content, design, delivery and assessment of academic learning both in school education as well as higher education through effective use of ICT tools.

4. Healthcare

Initiatives taken where innovative and sustainable ICT technologies have been developed to make healthcare facilities easily accessible at reasonable prices to both rural and urban masses. Focus can be on transformation projects, apps, telemedicine projects and others which has a made significant contribution to the health sector.

5. Agriculture

Initiatives undertaken which has helped in increasing agriculture yield of farmers in the given region through effective use of ICT. Focus is on innovation, ease of use and its impact on farmers for a given region.

6. Electronics Manufacturing

India is focusing on electronics manufacturing in a big way both on equipment as well as consumer segments like mobile phone and its accessories. Till date, more than 90 electronics manufacturing units have set up shops in India and more manufacturing units are planning to set up their units in the country. How startups are helping electronics manufacturing ecosystem.

7. ICE Services

There is lot of convergence happening in the space of ICE (IT, Communications and Entertainment) and in this category we are focusing on ICE startups who are helping India in the transformation process to become a digital and smart nation. Focus is on services provided and how is it impacting nation, state and district at large.

Evaluation Criteria

A detailed quantitative methodology has been developed to fairly and accurately benchmark and analyse each entry on its merits. The jury shall evaluate entries based on four criteria: Innovation, Exemplary Courage/Risk Taking, Display of Successful Implementation Skills and Impact - Customer/Social/Financial. Scoring is based on a scale of 1-5. 1 being the lowest and 5 being the highest.

1. Innovation and Technology (30%): The focus is on innovation and technology with respect to the product/solutions offered for a given problem. The focus is on uniqueness and novelty factor, maturity/stage of technology, investment made in the project, core team strength and manpower resources deployed. Wherever possible provides facts and figures substantiating the claims to give jury the most comprehensive and informed view.

2. Exemplary Courage/Risk Taking (20%): The focus is on how successful is the Business Model with respect to market risk, management risk and financial risk. And how the project can be scaled up in future. Wherever possible, provide facts and figures substantiating claims to give the jury the most comprehensive and informed view.

3. Display of Successful Implementation Skills (20%): The focus is on the type of projects completed which shows different skillsets of the startup. Also the feedback from the client with respect to successful implementation. Wherever possible, provide facts and figures substantiating claims to give the jury the most comprehensive and informed view.

4. Impact - Customer/Social/Financial/Economic (30%): The focus is on whether the solution or product has benefited or will benefit a large number of people. The focus is on parameters such as customer/social/financial/economic impact. The focus is also on economic gains due to digitisation. The note should explain how user or customer has been impacted or will impact, how society has been impacted or will impact and on the financial front the focus is on growth in turnover. Would request if you can provide last year audited balance sheet of FY2016-17.

Eligibility Criteria

1. The award is open to all Founders and Co-Founders of companies who are registered and operating in India under the Companies Act. Awards are person centric and will be given to IIM Alumni only.

2. The Founder or Co-founder should be a pass out of full time course of any of the 20 IIMs (Indian Institute of Management) located across India. This includes two year PGP, PGPABM, PGPPP, PGPSM, PGPX, EPGP, IPMX, WMP, FPM and EFPM courses.

3. There are two types of award categories in Startups - Promising Startups and Star Startups. Awards are person centric and will be given to IIM Alumni only. Founders or Co-Founders who are non-IIMs and may have contributed to the success will not be considered for the award.

4. For Promising Startups, the date of incorporation of startups should be less than or equal to 5 years as on October 31, 2017 ​or​ the total revenue generated in terms of turnover from the date of incorporation should be less than or equal to Rs. 50 Crore.

5. For Startup Stars, the date of incorporation of startups should be more than 5 years as on October 31, 2017 ​or​ the total revenue generated in terms of turnover from the date of incorporation should be more than Rs. 50 Crore. For Startup Stars, the new initiative should not be more than 10 years as on October 31, 2017.

6. The award is open to startup companies operating in the space of Citizen Services, Education, Healthcare, Financial Services, Agriculture, Electronics Manufacturing and ICE Services and are using ICT services. If we receive large number of nominations we will go for sectorwise awards.

7. The entries will be accepted only when it is submitted in English language.

8. The entries must be submitted online. Additional online documents are accepted for each category in one go only and not in batches.

9. There is no maximum number of entries permitted from startups. One can submit as many different entries to as many categories as one would like.

10. Any additional online documents are accepted for each category in one go and not in batches.

12. If entry or entries fail to meet the eligibility criteria set out, the entry or entires may be withdrawn from the awards competition. In the event of disqualification or withdrawal, startups will not be notified.
